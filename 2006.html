IITM Open Quiz 2011




Q1 When filling out internet forms specific to the US, what is the zip code most commonly given by non-Americans?

Answer
90210 ( From Beverly hills 90210)

Q2 Participants at the Visit Scotland Adventure Triathlon were provided an insurance cover of one million pound sterling each against a very unique occurrence. What?

Answer
Being attacked by the Loch Ness Monster

Q3 It is believed that this idiom - which alludes to the practice of taking from one entity in order to satisfy another - has its origin in 17th-century England, when part of the estate of Saint Peter's Cathedral in Westminster was appropriated to pay for repairs to Saint Paul's in London. Which phrase?

Answer
Robbing Peter to Pay Paul

Q4 This company was founded in 1923 under the name 'Ideal' . Its engineers used to mark goods which had passed their stringent quality tests in a certain way. Soon, the company began to be identified with the quality mark. How do we know this company today?

Answer
Blaupunkt

Q5 X was a person of Indo-Portuguese origin. He is considered to be a pioneer of the study of hypnotism built upon work done by other stalwarts like Franz Mesmer. In his youth, X studied theology and wanted to pursue a career in the clergy. In the late 1780s, X became involved in the Pintos conspiracy (an elaborate scheme to assassinate high members of the Goan Catholic Church), and had to flee to France. After a few years of quiet life, X played an active role in a small scale revolt in Marseille. The long arm of the law finally caught up with him and he was imprisoned in 1797. After his release several years later he met Y, who immortalized him in some way. Name X and Y.

Answer
Abbe Faria and Alexandre Dumas

Q6 X is an asteroid moon that was discovered by a team of French-Canadian astronomers in 1998. It orbits the larger asteroid Eugenia, which is named after the wife of Napoleon III. X was given its rather unique name in 2003, in the honour of another famous French citizen from an entirely unrelated field, though related to asteroids in a very special manner.What was the asteroid called ?

Answer
The Little Prince

Q7 Recently, a South Dakota-based food historian called Mary Gunderson created a new dish by modifying the content of an already existing dish X. She replaced the English muffin with a thick slice of German rye bread, and substituted a "weisswurst" sausage, a form of marinated roast beef, for the ham.

The complete list of ingredients are-
4 broiled wurst, such as weisswurst or bratwurst
4 slices hearty German rye bread, toasted and lightly buttered
8 poached eggs
Hollandaise sauce (recipe follows)
2/3 cup sweet and sour red cabbage
What did she call this dish?
Answer
Eggs Benedict XVI

Q8 Traditionally made during death ceremonies, but nowadays in most festivals in Tamil Nadu, a specific type of vadai called uddina vada is served. This is made of Urad Dal, which has one of the highest protein contents among all dals (100 gms of urad dhal has 24.0 gms of protein), and is thus one of the main protein sources of the meal. Historians today believe that this food item is proof of a certain change in the habits of Brahmins which took place around 500BC. What change, and what historic event was responsible for it?

Answer
The Advent of Jainism caused Brahmins to turn vegetarian. The Uddina vada was prepared to make up for protein lost due to the non-intake of meat.

Q9 The ________ theorem in the field of optimization states that all algorithms that try to find the maximum or minimum of a cost function perform exactly the same, when they are averaged over all possible cost functions. This is essentially because there is an inherent bias in each algorithm, as it tends to perform extremely well over a certain range, and then falls away rapidly over other cost functions outside its optimum range - thus averaging to a constant. The theorem gets its name from an adage popularized in 20th century literature , and is also used freely in economics. What is the theorem called?

Answer
The No Free Lunch Theorem

Q10 Several law colleges in USA had huge libraries with very extensive collection of law books. However, with the advent of the internet, the administrators of these colleges soon realized that there was a lot of useful material which could be easily accessed from the web. In order to encourage this, they set up internet terminals in their libraries. What did they name these terminals?

Answer
@tticus
